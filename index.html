Illustrating change at Mount Saint Peter

Mount Saint Peter is the highest 'mountain' in the Netherlands.
It lies in the near the city of Maastricht and is next to the Meuse river

Mount Saint Peter's limestone composition and its deposits of flint nodules and its geographic makes it a remarkable place
The locals have mined for flint there since the stone age!
The network of mining tunnels extended over 200 kilometers in the 19th century.
These days mining is not taking place at this cite anymore, and it is now considered an important area for recreation (Wikipedia).

In the pictures below different height maps of the mount are shown of 3 different periods of time (2002, 2012 and 2019).
The height of all points off the mountain are also shown within the three pictures. The legend can be found below the picture.

The difference between the height can be determined using the slider and selecting the year on each side:

In order to see how the mount looks like without elevation data the button 'ClearView' can be selected.
This slider helps in illustrating the change at Mount Saint Peter over time making comparison very clear for the viewer.

The data is gathered using the 3 AHN datasets with as a background the 8cm RGB aerial picture from pdok.
